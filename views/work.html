Major works created by Billy

1 - Billy Graham, an autobiography

The apostle Paul had said that man should be like an open letter in the eyes of God. The letter, or rather the book of Billy Graham's life, is an example of content. Billy Graham's biography became public when he was already 80 years old, when the minister was already retiring. His trajectory full of memorable stories and prestigious friendships is reported by himself, with all his orator's mental vigor. Many photographs chronicle Billy's successful saga, from the beginnings of preaching to the point that he became a presidential adviser, a position he held for years. Participation in the National Crusades in gymnasiums and stadiums, their effect on the expansion of the Baptist church, is all in this book. It is also remarkable to see how he dedicated himself to the cause and where he drew so much determination. Billy, as a servant of God, felt chosen early on, with this role to fulfill. When he started, he never stopped: from door-to-door salesman, he visited five continents, preaching in various regions and countries. He was present in riots and social upheavals such as Paris and Woodstock. One of the most emotional days of Billy Graham's journey is also remembered, when he made the crusade through New York, being broadcast to two million faithful in 21 states of America. His relationship with figures such as Eisenhower and Bill Clinton is reported, as well as dedicating space in the book to give importance to anonymous people who have touched him, such as close relatives and even death row prisoners, to whom he can alleviate some of the martyrdom. It is not for nothing that this apostle who lived 99 years and dedicated his life to a cause is so remembered.

2. Peace with God

The words of comfort in this book are organized to lead you to look at life as if you were sailing on the calm sea after the storm. They are words of comfort and affection that help to understand the meaning of all the back-and-forth of life that pulsates within us. The sooner we become aware of this ocean of doubts and changes, and learn to balance with it, the less suffering, hurt and anger. Happiness is accessible to everyone in the form of understanding. If you want to call something mundane the name of happiness, then you will have to face the danger of not getting it – or losing it. “Peace with God” is a book to trust in God and in yourself and to accept life in its full exercise.

3. The reason for my hope: resilience and perseverance to never give up

The author will show how he deals with the issue of resilience in his life, also citing other examples that serve to motivate the faithful. See that "faithful" is not necessarily the follower of some pastor or covenant, but the person faithful to himself, to what he believes. Being faithful to your plan and not doubting it is necessary in order not to feed the ghost of giving up. Giving up doesn't come overnight. It is the result of an accumulation of moments of weakness and we must be alert to everything that throws us back into laziness, comfort, inertia and pessimism. An important book for the countless cases of anxiety and depression that are plaguing contemporary society.
