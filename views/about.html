Who was Billy Graham?

Billy Graham was a pastor, evangelist, preacher and one of the greatest revivalists who have passed through history. Certainly one of the pastors who marked the history of the Church. Known for his straightforward sermons and accessible gospels, Billy preached to a mass audience of nearly 2.5 million people in live audiences in 185 countries and territories on six continents. In addition, he was an advisor to several US presidents such as John F. Kennedy, Richard Nixon, Ronald Reagan and Bill Clinton, even being called “the Pastor of America” by past presidents. It was an example of conduct, submission and complete dedication to the Gospel. In your own words: “Some Christians have learned very little about what it is to have a daily devotional. Some time ago, a policeman asked me what it was the secret to having a victorious life. I told you there is no formula magic that can articulate it. But if there was a word that could to describe the victorious life, I would say it is submission. The second word I would use is devotion. Nothing can replace the devotional life daily with Christ. Get down on your knees and pray until you and God come together become close friends”’ – Billy Graham.
